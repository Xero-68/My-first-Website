What It's Like to Have a Hearing Disability

Living with a hearing disability can affect communication, relationships, work, and social interactions...

Types of Hearing Loss

  • Conductive Hearing Loss: A problem in the outer or middle ear...
  • Causes: Ear infections, Fluid in the middle ear...
  • Sensorineural Hearing Loss: Caused by damage to the inner ear...
  • Causes: Aging, Noise Exposure...
  • Mixed Hearing Loss: A combination of conductive and sensorineural hearing loss.
  • Causes: Head trauma, Chronic Ear infection...
  • Auditory Neuropathy Spectrum Disorder (ANSD): Sound enters the ear but signal transmission is impaired.
  • Causes: Premature birth, Damage to the auditory nerve...

Types of Hearing Aids

  • Behind-the-Ear (BTE): Sits behind the ear...
  • In-the-Ear (ITE): Custom-fit into the outer ear...
  • In-the-Canal (ITC) & Completely-in-Canal (CIC): Smaller, more discreet...
  • Receiver-in-Canal (RIC): Similar to BTE but smaller...

Assistive Devices for Noisy Environments

In loud or crowded settings, hearing aids alone might not be enough...

  • FM Systems: Transmit speaker’s voice directly...
  • Personal FM: One-on-one; speaker wears transmitter...
  • Soundfield FM: Broadcasts to room speakers...
  • Loop Systems: Use electromagnetic fields...
  • Room Loop, Counter Loop, Portable Loops
  • Captioning Apps: Real-time text transcription...
  • Closed and Open Captioning, CART, Live Apps (Ava, Otter.ai...)
  • Remote Microphones: Clipped to a speaker...
  • Bluetooth and dedicated mics like Roger Pen...

How to Support a Deaf Student or Coworker

Deaf individuals may use different ways to communicate...

1. Communication Tips

  • Ask their preferred communication method...
  • Maintain eye contact and speak clearly...

2. Classroom or Workplace Strategies

  • Provide written summaries...
  • Allow seating with clear view of the speaker...

3. Be Inclusive and Respectful

  • Don’t assume—ask what they need.
  • Face them when speaking...

Remember: Accessibility benefits everyone...

Supporting a Student or Coworker with Single-Sided Deafness

SSD means hearing in one ear only...

  • Always speak on their hearing side...
  • Reduce background noise...
  • Use clear speech, visible lips...

Final Note: Always ask the person what works best...
